Nadezhda Udaltsova, 1916

Untitled

Nadezhda Andreevna Udaltsova was born in the village of Orel in 1886. Udaltsova's professional debut was as a participant in a Jack of Diamonds exhibition in Moscow in the winter of 1914.[5] But it was in 1915 that she really made her name as a Cubist artist, participating in three major exhibitions in that single year. Under the influence of Tatlin, Udaltsova experimented with Constructivism, but eventually embraced the more painterly approach of the Suprematist movement.
